Automated decision making/profiling

 

Jifflr undertakes profiling on Consumers.

 

The Jifflr App is a market research tool intended to obtain useful and truthful feedback from users to help businesses, organisations and communities better serve their customers or stakeholder groups.  The usefulness of the user feedback will increase if the respondents have an interest or some other association with the material requiring feedback.  To help with directing market research to the appropriate audience, the ‘consumer’ user data entered into the App (at registration, during a monthly ‘quiz’, prompted by Jifflr to update or improve its user’s profile, or when providing responses to market research questions) will be used in manual and/or automated processes to develop a detailed user profile and ensure that any market research presented to a user is likely to be of interest, although this cannot be guaranteed.

 

If Your registration data or in-App market research responses are not truthful and honest it is likely that You will be presented with market research products that are of little interest to you.  Not only will this impact Your in-App user experience, but it may affect the quality of your responses returned to third parties who pay Us to pay You to undertake the market research for them.  Poor quality responses will mean that any responses have little intrinsic value and would undermine the Jifflr business model.  To mitigate this risk automated processes examine user data, including answers to market research questions, to identify inconsistencies that may indicate inaccurate user data or responses that are either not truthful or they are in regular conflict. 

 

This automated analysis may result in recommendations that user accounts are suspended or closed and in the most serious cases this process may automatically suspend or close user accounts. You have a right to object to this automated process, however by doing so we will no longer be able to provide you with our services.

INFORMATION ABOUT OUR USE OF COOKIES AND IP ADDRESSES

 

We may collect information about your mobile phone, computer or other device from which you use our mobile App or access Our Website including where available your IP address, operating system and browser type, for systems administration, data security, App functionality and to report aggregate anonymised information to third parties affiliates. This is statistical data about our users’ App use and browsing actions and patterns, and does not identify any individual. We may, however, use such information in conjunction with the data We have about you in order to record your usage of our services.

 

Our Website uses cookies to distinguish you from other users of our Website. This helps Us to provide you with a good experience when you browse our Website and also allows Us to improve the Website. By using our Website you agree to our use of cookies as more specifically set out below.

 

A cookie is a small file of letters and numbers that We store on your browser or the hard drive of your computer if you agree. Cookies contain information that is transferred to your computer's hard drive.

 

The cookies We use include:

 

·       “Analytical” cookies. They help Us to improve the way our Website works by allowing Us to recognise and count the number of visitors and to see how visitors move around the Website when they are using it.  They help to ensure that users are finding what they are looking for easily.

  • “Strictly necessary” cookies. They enable you to log into secure areas of our Website, use a shopping cart or make use of e-billing services etc. These cookies are required for the operation of our Website.
  • “Functionality” cookies. These enable Us to personalise our content for you, greet you by name and remember your preferences (for example, your choice of language or region).  They are used to recognise you when you return to our Website.
  • “Targeting” cookies. These cookies are used to make our Website, offers e-mailed to you and the advertising displayed on it more relevant to your interests.  We may also share this information with third parties for this purpose. These cookies record your visit to our Website, the pages you have visited and the links you have followed to our affiliates websites.  

Please note that third parties affiliates may also use cookies, over which We have no control.

 

You may block cookies by activating the setting on your browser that allows you to refuse the setting of all or some cookies. However, if you use your browser settings to block all cookies (including essential cookies) you may not be able to access all or parts of the Website. Unless you have adjusted your browser setting so that it will refuse cookies, our system will issue cookies as soon as you visit our Website.
